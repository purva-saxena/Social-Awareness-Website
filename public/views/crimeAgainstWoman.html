Complaint & Investigation

Register the complaint offline or online as per your convinience. For registering your complaint for crime against women online, visit official website of National Commission for Women . The National Commission for Women (NCW) is the statutory body of the Government of India, generally concerned with advising the government on all policy matters affecting women. It also deals with the complaints received from all over the country including those relating to deprivation of rights of women and involving injustice to women takes suo motu cognizance of incidents related to commission of heinous crimes against women U/S 10 of the National Commission Act, 1990. For offline complaint, visit your nearby Police station.

Here are the steps for registering your complaint online :-
  1. Visit the Website of NCW by using the link http://ncwapps.nic.in/
  2. Click on Register Complaint Button
  3. Click on Complaint Registration Button
  4. Fill the Details of Complainant including Name, Address, State, District, PIN Code, E-mail, Mobile No, Sex etc.
  5. Fill Details of Victim including Whether Complainant is Victim (Yes or No), Name, Address, STate, District, PIN Code, E-mail, Mobile No, Sex, Date of Birth, Religion, Caster, Whether mentally or physically Challenged or not
  6. Fill Details of Respondent (Opposite Party) including Name, Address, State, District, PIN Code, E-mail, Mobile No, Sex
  7. Insert Complete Details of incident
  8. Click on Submit Button After completing this process your complaint will be submitted with the National Commission for Women. You will get online receipt for from NCW. This receipt number can be used to track complaint status with National Commission for Women.
Scrutiny of Complaints

The complaints received are scrutinised as per Commission’s mandate and adopted procedure to handle complaints The complaints which falls within the Commission’s mandate and adopted procedure to handle complaints are registered under 19 identified categories

The complaints of the following nature are summarily dismissed:-
  • Complaints which are illegible or vague, anonymous or pseudonymous
  • The issue raised relates to civil dispute between the parties
  • The issue raised relates to service matters
  • Matter is sub judice before a Court/Tribunal
  • Complaints which are already pending before a State Commission or any other Commission
  • Complaints only endorsed to the Commission
  • Complaints involving no deprivation of women rights
